What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. These attacks often come through emails, messages, fake websites, or even phone calls. The goal is to steal sensitive data such as login credentials, bank details, or personal identity information.

Why It Matters

  • 📊 95% of data breaches involve successful phishing attacks
  • 💰 $4.91 million average cost per data breach in 2023
  • ⏱️ Every 39 seconds a cyber attack occurs globally
  • 🎯 1 in 4 employees will click on phishing links
  • 🛡️ Phishing bypasses even advanced security systems, making human awareness critical.

Social Impact

  • 🏥 Healthcare disruption: Patient data theft, service outages
  • 🏫 Education targets: Student records, research data at risk
  • 👥 Personal consequences: Identity theft, financial loss
  • 🏢 Business impact: Reputation damage, legal liability
  • 🌍 Community risk: Compromised systems can spread malware across networks.

Learn the Fundamentals

Types of Phishing

  • 📧 Email Phishing: Mass emails to steal credentials
  • 🎯 Spear Phishing: Targeted attacks on specific individuals
  • 🐋 Whaling: Targeting high-profile executives
  • 📱 Smishing: Phishing via SMS/text messages
  • 📞 Vishing: Voice/phone-based phishing attacks

Red Flags to Watch

  • ⚡ Urgency: "Act now or lose access!"
  • 🔤 Poor grammar: Spelling/grammar mistakes
  • 🔗 Suspicious links: Hover to reveal true destination
  • 📎 Unexpected attachments: Especially .exe, .zip files
  • 👤 Generic greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your name

Prevention Tips

  • ✅ Verify sender: Contact organization directly
  • 🔐 Use 2FA: Enable two-factor authentication
  • 🔄 Keep updated: Update software regularly
  • 📚 Security training: Stay informed about new threats
  • 🛡️ Email filters: Use spam/phishing filters

Email Security Basics

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)

Validates that emails are sent from authorized servers for the domain.

v=spf1 include:_spf.example.com ~all

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)

Uses cryptographic signatures to verify email hasn't been tampered with.

DKIM-Signature: v=1; a=rsa-sha256; d=example.com...

DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)

Combines SPF and DKIM to provide instructions for handling failed authentication.

v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@example.com

Sample Suspicious Email Header

Here's what a suspicious email header might look like:

From: security@paypaI.com (note the capital 'i' instead of 'l')
Reply-To: noreply@suspicious-domain.com
Return-Path: <bounce@malicious-server.net>
Received: from unknown-server.xyz ([192.168.1.100])
Subject: URGENT: Verify Your Account Now!
Date: Thu, 1 Jan 2024 25:99:99 +0000 (invalid timestamp)

Red flags: Domain spoofing, mismatched reply-to, suspicious server, impossible timestamp

How to Report Phishing

  1. Don't click anything in the suspicious email
  2. Forward the email to your IT security team
  3. Report to authorities: Forward to phishing@fbi.gov or your local CERT
  4. Mark as spam/phishing in your email client
  5. Delete the email after reporting
  6. Inform colleagues if it's a targeted company attack
